MY NNE NNE.

1972 - 2021

World best mum.

I thought about you again today. It's still strange how you've come to me just once- A very sad "once"
I haven't forgiven you yet- for dying, even though I know you fought. A part of me thinks we'd have won if we fought harder. I want you to say "Rapu onye ala a" and smile sweetly when I make some of my silly jokes or be sarcastic.
I want to see you again, I want to yell at you when I'm upset, grumble when you send me on errands, laugh alike and so loudly with you in a way that some people would say "Kamsy yichasialu Nneka, oyifokwia" I feel bad that I don't grieve enough, I feel like I should sit and cry everyday and never move on but alas, I don't.
Does this mean I didn't love you enough?

My Nne nne was:

  1. Lovable.
      2. She was a person full of love and who everyone loved.
  2. Beautiful.
      3. She was really beautiful and tall too.
  3. Smart.
      4. My Nne nne was really smart, we were always in awe of how quickly she could provide solutions.
  4. Generous to a fault.
    5. Everyone could testify of her generosity, She was always willing to give and give.
  5. Brave.
      6. Nne was brave and fearless. She hated bullies and would always stand up for anyone who was being bullied.

May her beautiful soul rest in God's bosom.
